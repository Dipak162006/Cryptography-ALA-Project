What is SHA?

Secure Hash Algorithm (SHA) is a family of cryptographic hash functions that maps data of any size to a fixed-size bit string. It acts like a digital fingerprint for files or messages.

What is the Avalanche Effect?

"Even a small change in input produces a completely different hash output." This property ensures that any tampering drastically changes the output, making manipulation mathematically impossible to hide.

Why is hashing important in digital signatures?

Digital signatures are applied to hashes, not full files. Hashing guarantees speed and consistency, while the avalanche effect ensures total data integrity.
